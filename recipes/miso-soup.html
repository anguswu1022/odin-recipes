Miso Soup Recipe

Description

Originating from Japan, miso soup is a staple dish. There are many variations of the soup, as people may decide to add in different ingredients.

Miso soup is made with the use of dashi, which is a stock made of dried kelp and dried bonito, and miso paste. There are red, white, yellow miso paste and any one of them is fine. You may choose to add in other add-ons to add to the soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp. dashi granules
  • 4 cups of water
  • 3 tbsp choice of miso paste
  • 1 (8 oz.) package of sliced silken tofu
  • 2 green onions, sliced into half-inch pieces

Steps

  1. Add the 4 cups of water to a pot and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. As the water heats up, slowly incorporate the dashi granules.
  3. Once the dashi granules is mixed evenly in water, reduce the heat to medium and whisk in the miso paste.
  4. Put in the tofu gently and add the separated green onions into the soup.
  5. Simmer gently for 2-3 minutes before serving.
